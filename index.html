Dr. Rajani Battu

MS, DNB, FRCS (Edin),
Fellow, Vitreoretina, Sankara Nethralaya, Chennai.
ICO fellow, Moorfields Eye Hospital, London.
PhD (Maastricht University)

Dr Rajani Battu is a senior consultant in the department of Ophthalmology at Aster CMI Hospital, Bangalore. Following her MBBS from Bangalore Medical College, she did her Master’s at St. John’s Medical College, Bangalore. She obtained her fellowship from the Royal College of Surgeons, Edinburgh. She underwent fellowship training in vitreo-retinal surgery at Sankara Nethralaya, Chennai. She has won multiple gold medals during her MBBS. She was awarded the AFER/ARVO visiting researcher award in 2009 in the US. She was awarded the best paper at the Karnataka Ophthalmic Society meet held in Mysuru in 2014. She has made several presentations at national and international conferences; she has many scientific publications to her credit. 

Rajani was awarded an International Council of Ophthalmology (ICO) fellowship with Prof Anthony Moore of Moorfields Eye Hospital, London, UK in ‘Inherited retinal dystrophies’. This started her journey with genetic diseases of the eye.

With an aim to further research and treatment into these conditions that have no treatment hitherto, Dr Rajani has started the ‘Centre for Eye Genetics and Research’ a not-for-profit organization that is dedicated to patients with genetic diseases of the eye. She is also a
Co-founder and Chief Medical Officer of Eyestem, a company dedicated to retinal stem cell research. Eyestem is currently one of the foremost companies in the country in this field.
Dr. Rajani hopes that this research will one day be helpful in treating patients in India.  

Dr. Rajani was recently awarded a PhD from Maastricht University, the Netherlands for her work on “Inherited Retinal Diseases”.
